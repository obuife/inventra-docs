# StockSense — Demo Video Script

**Target Runtime:** 3 minutes 30 seconds (3:30)
**Format:** Screen recording of Android app + voiceover
**Voiceover Language:** English (Nigerian accent preferred)
**Background Music:** Upbeat, professional — fade under VO. No lyrics.
**Audience:** Prospective SME users, investors, pilot programme participants
**Screen Resolution:** 1080p minimum (phone screen mirroring)
**Captions:** Yes — English closed captions on all VO lines
**Branding:** StockSense logo in top-right corner throughout. Outro card with website and download link.

---

## Column Guide

| Column | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Visual** | What appears on screen — what the camera/screen recording should show |
| **Voiceover (VO)** | The exact words the narrator speaks — record from this column |
| **Production Action** | Instructions for the video editor or on-screen graphics |

---

## Full Script

### Scene 01 — 0:00–0:15 | The Problem

**Visual:** Busy Nigerian retail shop. A woman stands at the counter looking stressed, surrounded by notebook ledgers and WhatsApp messages on her phone.

**Voiceover:**
> Every day, millions of Nigerian shop owners run their businesses from memory, notebooks, and WhatsApp. They lose stock without knowing it. They run out of products while customers are standing right in front of them.

**Production Action:** Cinematic B-roll footage of busy Lagos market or retail shop. Warm tones. Narrator voice enters at 0:02. Fade in StockSense logo bottom-right at 0:10.

---

### Scene 02 — 0:15–0:25 | Brand Reveal

**Visual:** Screen fades to black. StockSense logo animates in from centre. Tagline appears below: *"Know your stock. Run your business."*

**Voiceover:**
> Introducing StockSense. The smart, simple inventory app built for Nigerian businesses.

**Production Action:** Logo animation — 1 second fade-in. Tagline types on screen after logo lands. Music rises slightly. Hold for 2 seconds.

---

### Scene 03 — 0:25–0:45 | Onboarding

**Visual:** Android phone showing the StockSense login screen. Hand taps phone number field, types a number. OTP notification arrives. User enters OTP. Business name screen appears — user types "Mama Ngozi Stores." Dashboard loads.

**Voiceover:**
> Getting started takes less than 3 minutes. Enter your phone number, receive a one-time code by SMS, set your business name, and you're in. No email. No complicated forms. Just your phone number.

**Production Action:** Screen recording, close-up of phone. Highlight each tap with a subtle circle. Show OTP SMS notification appearing. Speed up slightly if needed to fit 20 seconds.

---

### Scene 04 — 0:45–1:05 | Dashboard

**Visual:** Dashboard screen. The personalised greeting reads: *"Good morning, Ngozi. You have 2 items running low."* Summary cards show today's sales, stock status (amber), and active alerts.

**Voiceover:**
> Your dashboard tells you exactly what is happening in your business, every morning, at a glance. Today's sales. Low stock warnings. The items that need your attention — all in one screen.

**Production Action:** Highlight the greeting text with a subtle glow or underline. Highlight each card in sequence as VO mentions it. Slow, deliberate pacing.

---

### Scene 05 — 1:05–1:25 | Inventory

**Visual:** Inventory screen. Product list shows items with colour-coded stock badges: green, amber, red. Narrator taps one product — *Dettol Soap 135g*. Product detail shows: current stock 4, reorder level 10, low stock badge in red.

**Voiceover:**
> StockSense tracks every product in real time. Green means you have enough. Amber means reorder soon. Red means act now. Every product shows exactly how many units are left and what your reorder level is.

**Production Action:** Animate colour-coded badges as they are mentioned. Zoom into product detail screen for last 5 seconds.

---

### Scene 06 — 1:25–1:45 | Recording a Sale

**Visual:** New Sale screen. Attendant's hand types "Indomie" in the search bar. Product appears instantly. Attendant taps it — quantity is 1. Taps **Confirm Sale**. Animated green tick: *"Sale Recorded."* Screen clears instantly.

**Voiceover:**
> Recording a sale takes 3 taps. Search for the product, confirm the quantity, and tap confirm. Stock updates automatically. Your attendant is ready for the next customer in seconds.

**Production Action:** Highlight each tap with a circle. Animated tick should be satisfying — bouncy, confident. Show full screen clear at the end. Entire sequence in under 20 seconds.

---

### Scene 07 — 1:45–2:05 | Attendant Accountability

**Visual:** Sales list. Owner filters by attendant: *"Amaka."* Sales appear with product names, quantities, times, amounts. Owner taps one sale, sees full detail.

**Voiceover:**
> As the owner, you can see every sale, by every staff member, for any time period. You see exactly who sold what, when, and for how much. No more guessing. No more stories.

**Production Action:** Slow pan through the sales list. Emphasise the attendant name column with a gentle highlight. End on the detail view of one transaction.

---

### Scene 08 — 2:05–2:25 | Alerts & Reordering

**Visual:** Alerts screen. Two alerts visible: *"Dettol Soap — Low Stock: 4 remaining, threshold: 10"* and *"Paracetamol Syrup — Expiring in 14 days."* Narrator taps first alert, taps **Create Purchase Order**. PO screen appears pre-filled.

**Voiceover:**
> StockSense warns you before problems happen. Low stock? You get an alert. A product expiring soon? You get an alert at 30, 14, and 7 days before the date. One tap creates a purchase order pre-filled with your supplier's details.

**Production Action:** Each alert card clearly visible. Highlight the alert type icons. Show PO creation briefly — enough to communicate the pre-fill.

---

### Scene 09 — 2:25–2:45 | Offline Mode

**Visual:** Network bar shows no signal. App shows **"Offline Mode"** ribbon at the top. Attendant records a sale. Sale records. Sync badge appears: *"1 sale waiting to sync."* Network signal returns. Sync badge disappears with a tick.

**Voiceover:**
> Even without internet, StockSense keeps working. Sales are saved on the phone and sync automatically when your connection returns. No data is ever lost.

**Production Action:** Show the offline ribbon clearly. Emphasise the sync badge number. Make the sync completion satisfying — a small tick animation. This is a key differentiator — give it full screen time.

---

### Scene 10 — 2:45–3:05 | Reports

**Visual:** Reports screen. Owner selects *Sales Report, This Week.* Summary cards appear: total revenue, top product, transaction count. Owner taps **Export → PDF**. Share sheet appears. Owner taps **WhatsApp**.

**Voiceover:**
> At the end of the week, generate your sales report in seconds. See your total revenue, your best-selling products, and your staff performance. Export to PDF and share directly with your accountant on WhatsApp.

**Production Action:** Show report cards populating quickly. Highlight the Export button. End with the WhatsApp share action — recognisable to every Nigerian viewer.

---

### Scene 11 — 3:05–3:25 | All Personas

**Visual:** Smooth transitions between four shots — shop owner checking dashboard remotely, attendant recording sale, manager running reconciliation, pharmacy owner seeing expiry alert.

**Voiceover:**
> StockSense is for every Nigerian business — retail shops, pharmacies, wholesalers, mini-marts. Whether you're the owner, the manager, or the attendant on the counter, StockSense helps you do your job better.

**Production Action:** Each persona gets 4–5 seconds. Use real-looking phone screen recordings. Music swells slightly approaching the outro.

---

### Scene 12 — 3:25–3:35 | Outro

**Visual:** StockSense logo centred on screen. Tagline: *"Know your stock. Run your business."* Download button / web URL appears below the logo.

**Voiceover:**
> StockSense. Know your stock. Run your business. Download now.

**Production Action:** Hold logo for 10 seconds. Animate tagline in. Show download URL or QR code below. Music fades out gracefully.

## Narrator Notes

| Guidance | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Warm, confident, and relatable. Talking to a Nigerian shop owner, not a tech conference. Conversational, not corporate. |
| **Pace** | Steady and clear. Slightly slower than natural speech to allow visuals to breathe. No rushing. |
| **Accent** | Nigerian English preferred. Do not adopt a generic Western commercial voice. |
| **Key words to emphasise** | "exactly", "automatically", "instantly", "3 taps", "even without internet", "never lost" |
| **Pauses** | Pause 0.5 seconds after each key benefit before continuing. Let the viewer absorb. |
| **Energy arc** | Build from S01 (empathy) → S06–S09 (excitement) → S11–S12 (inspiring close) |

## Pre-Recording Device Checklist

- [ ] Demo account name: "Mama Ngozi Stores"
- [ ] At least 20 products loaded — 2 below reorder level, 1 expiring in 14 days
- [ ] Attendant account "Amaka" with 3–5 sales already recorded
- [ ] Supplier linked to at least one low-stock product
- [ ] Phone: full battery, Do Not Disturb ON, all notifications hidden except StockSense
- [ ] Screen recording software ready (1080p minimum)
- [ ] Offline scenario tested in airplane mode before final take
